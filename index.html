COSC 4397: Generative AI in Practice

Overview

This course provides a practical, hands-on introduction to Generative AI and Large Language Models (LLMs) such as Gemini, chatGPT, Claude, and open-source models like Gemma, LLaMA and leverage the technology to build real world AI applications on top of LLMs. The course is introductory, generic and requires background in programming and analytical thinking/Math. It can also serve students with different sub majors from various disciplines—including the humanities, law, psychology, and biomedical sciences— who wish to understand, apply, and critically evaluate generative AI tools in real-world contexts. Through guided lectures, codebase demonstration, and projects, students will explore prompt engineering, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and model alignment concepts like Reinforcement Learning with Human Feedback (RLHF), Proximal Policy, Direct Preference Optimization (PPO, DPO), Fine tuning and adaptation (PEFT, LoRA) focusing on intuition and application rather than math-heavy theory. The course integrates ethical considerations throughout, addressing algorithmic bias, fairness, transparency, accountability, and responsible AI deployment. Hands-on homeworks and a final capstone project will enable students to design, build, and evaluate practical generative AI applications relevant to their interests while adhering to ethical guidelines and responsible innovation principles.
